Soba Noodle Ramen Recipe

Total Time

Prep Time

Cook Time

30 min

15 min

15 min

Description

A homemade version of ramen using buckwheat noodles and fresh veggies as a healthier alternative to instant ramen.

Instructions

  1. Heat up the broth in quart pan
    1. Add the cloves of garlic, sliced ginger, and soy sauce to the broth.
    2. Bring the broth to a light boil. Let the broth simmer while you prepare the rest of the ingredients.
  2. In a non stick pan, heat up a tablespoon of oil and add the fresh spinach. Cook for a couple of minutes until wilted. Lightly salt the wilted spinach. Set aside.
  3. Cook the soba noodles according to the package directions. Once the noodles are done, rinse the noodles under cold water. Set aside.
  4. To assemble, add the cooked soba noodles to a soup bowl.
    1. Ladle about two cups of the broth into the bowl.
    2. Add the wilted spinach, shredded carrots, chopped scallions and the sliced hard boiled egg on top of the noodles.

Additional Toppings

  1. If you like things on the spicy side, try adding a teaspoon or two of garlic, chili sauce or sriracha.
  2. You can add just about any type of your favorite veggies on top of your soup. Try mushrooms, corn, and other types of fresh greens like kale or bok choy.

Ingredients

  • 8 cups Chicken/Vegetable Broth
  • 1 inch Fresh Ginger, sliced
  • 4 cloves Garlic, smashed
  • 1 bunch Scallions, chopped
  • 1 bunch Spinach
  • 1 cup Carrots, shredded
  • 2 Hard Boiled Eggs, sliced in half
  • 1/4 cup Soy Sauce
  • 6 oz Dry Soba Noodles
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • Salt & Pepper to taste
